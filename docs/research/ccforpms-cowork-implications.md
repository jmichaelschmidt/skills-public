# Cowork Implications

This note captures what changed in the starter-pack because of the Cowork research and what did not.

## Bottom Line

The research did not justify changing the starter-pack core role set.

It did justify:

- making `done`, `context`, and `constraints` explicit in the shared briefing contract
- strengthening file and artifact workflow guidance for non-code work
- adding reusable skills for research synthesis and artifact handling

## What Stayed The Same

### The core six roles stayed

The public starter-pack still uses:

- `planner`
- `implementer`
- `reviewer`
- `security-reviewer`
- `docs-handoff`
- `repo-hygiene`

The research supported better briefs and better sidecars, not a bigger default team chart.

### Serial-by-default stayed

The starter-pack still prefers one active write thread at a time, with narrow parallelism reserved for:

- review
- audit
- research
- synthesis

## What Changed

### Briefing now centers on done, context, and constraints

The most important shared change was teaching the core prompts to expect:

- a concrete definition of done
- required context or read-first material
- explicit constraints such as scope, deletion rules, destination paths, naming, or move limits

### Artifact-heavy work got an optional workspace contract

The starter-pack now documents an advisory `inbox / processed / outputs / reference` pattern for non-code work. It is optional, but it gives teams a repeatable way to manage files when the output is more than a patch.

### Research synthesis got a stronger output standard

For research-heavy work, the design now expects durable outputs to preserve:

- sources
- contradictions
- unanswered questions
- the line between extracted facts and inference

### Optional skills beat more default roles

The changes were better expressed as reusable skills than as permanent team seats:

- research synthesis
- workspace artifact operations

## Design Consequence

The public starter-pack became stronger by improving the briefing contract and expanding reusable workflow primitives, not by turning into a larger role taxonomy.
